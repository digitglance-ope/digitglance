Project Overview

This document contains the complete research brief and content framework for the Digitglance Industry-Specific Excel Templates project. It covers all 11 target industries, with 5 practical, business-focused Excel templates developed for each industry — a total of 55 templates.

For each template, this document provides a clear description, a list of key features and functions, a practical use case drawn from real small and medium business scenarios, and an explanation of how the template improves business efficiency and supports better decision-making.

Each industry section concludes with an Economic Relevance summary covering five key dimensions: financial control, compliance and record-keeping, decision-making support, productivity and profitability, and the template's contribution to the wider economy.

🏗  Construction
Templates for project cost control, labour, billing, materials, and equipment management.

#1	Project Cost Tracker

Tracks all project costs — materials, labour, and overheads — from start to finish against an approved budget.
KEY FEATURES
•	Budget vs. actual cost comparison per phase
•	Cost category breakdown (civil, M&E, finishes)
•	Variance and overrun alerts with colour flags
•	Project completion percentage indicator
•	Summary dashboard by project phase
PRACTICAL USE CASE
A small construction firm managing a housing development tracks material costs and labour against the approved budget weekly, flagging any phase that exceeds its allocation before overruns become unrecoverable.
BUSINESS EFFICIENCY
Prevents budget overruns before they become critical. Gives project managers clear visibility into cost performance at every stage of the build, enabling timely corrective action.

#2	Material Usage Log

Records materials received, consumed, and remaining on-site for each project phase.
KEY FEATURES
•	Material entry by category (concrete, steel, timber)
•	Site allocation and phase tracking columns
•	Closing stock auto-calculation
•	Supplier reference and delivery date tracking
•	Low-stock alert flag for reordering
PRACTICAL USE CASE
The site manager logs daily cement, steel, and timber usage to prevent theft, reduce over-ordering, and plan deliveries accurately throughout the project.
BUSINESS EFFICIENCY
Reduces material waste and unplanned procurement. Maintains site accountability and supports accurate project cost reporting to clients and management.

#3	Labour Cost Sheet

Captures worker attendance, hours worked, daily rates, and total payroll costs per project.
KEY FEATURES
•	Worker roster with trade or skill classification
•	Daily hours entry per worker
•	Rate-per-hour or day with overtime calculation
•	Weekly and monthly payroll totals
•	Project-to-date labour cost summary
PRACTICAL USE CASE
A contractor calculates how much to pay casual labourers on a road construction project, breaking down costs by trade, week, and project phase for accurate billing.
BUSINESS EFFICIENCY
Ensures accurate payroll, reduces worker disputes, and keeps labour costs visible and traceable within the overall project budget for client reporting.

#4	Contract Billing Tracker

Manages progress billing for contracts, tracking amounts invoiced, received, and outstanding.
KEY FEATURES
•	Milestone billing schedule with due dates
•	Invoice number and date tracking
•	Payment received and outstanding balance columns
•	Retention amount calculation
•	Overdue invoice highlight and follow-up notes
PRACTICAL USE CASE
A construction company tracks payments from a government client across 6 billing milestones, ensuring invoices are raised on time and late payments are followed up promptly.
BUSINESS EFFICIENCY
Improves cash flow management, reduces payment delays, and supports contract compliance with clear documentation of every billing event throughout the project lifecycle.

#5	Equipment Maintenance Log

Records scheduled and unscheduled maintenance for all construction equipment including costs and next service dates.
KEY FEATURES
•	Equipment register with make, model, and serial number
•	Last and next service date tracking
•	Repair cost log per incident
•	Maintenance type (preventive vs. corrective)
•	Status column (operational, in-repair, retired)
PRACTICAL USE CASE
The fleet manager schedules preventive maintenance for excavators and concrete mixers, reducing the risk of unexpected breakdowns that stall the site and increase project costs.
BUSINESS EFFICIENCY
Extends equipment lifespan, lowers emergency repair costs, and prevents the project delays caused by unplanned equipment downtime — protecting both margins and delivery schedules.

Economic Relevance
Financial Control	Tracks actual vs. budgeted spend at every project stage, preventing cost overruns that erode contractor margins.
Compliance & Record Keeping	Provides documented records for client audits, tax inspections, and contractual billing disputes.
Better Decision-Making	Real-time cost data enables project managers to make timely decisions on resource reallocation, procurement, and scope changes.
Productivity & Profitability	Reduces time spent on manual tracking and reconciliation, freeing up site managers and accountants to focus on project delivery.
Contribution to Wider Economy	Better-managed construction SMEs complete projects on time and on budget, contributing more reliably to infrastructure development and employment.
